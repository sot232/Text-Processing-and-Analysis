id="Music">Music</span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Music_of_Korea" title="Music of Korea">Music of Korea</a></div>
<p>Traditional Korean music includes combinations of the folk, vocal, religious and <a href="/wiki/Korean_Court_Music" class="mw-redirect" title="Korean Court Music">ritual</a> music styles of the <a href="/wiki/Korean_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Korean people">Korean people</a>. Korean music has been practiced since prehistoric times.<sup id="cite_ref-167" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-167">[166]</a></sup> Korean music falls into two broad categories. The first, <a href="/wiki/Hyangak" title="Hyangak">Hyangak</a>, literally means <i>The local music</i> or <i>Music native to Korea</i> of which example is <i>Sujecheon</i>, a piece of instrumental music as old as 1,300 years.<sup id="cite_ref-168" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-168">[167]</a></sup> The second, <i>yangak</i>, represent a more Western style.</p>
